Origins

The origins of nuclear weapons span back to september 12, 1933 when Leo szilárd thought about a possible chain reaction to release and harness the energy stored in atoms. Years later Lise Meitner realized the process she later coined “nuclear fission” with Uranium. After this discovery however there began an increase in fear of Nazi Germany using this new found discovery to create nuclear weapons due to their sudden stockpile of uranium. This led to the creation of the famous or infamous Manhattan Project Group which started somewhere by the end of summer 1942. The Group[pictured above] (holding members such as the famous Oppenheimer and Albert Einstein among others) worked on this project till their successful creation and devastating implementation on july 16 1945. This was when the first atomic bomb was tested. A little less than a month later on august 6, 1945 the nuclear warhead the Little Boy was dropped on Hiroshima by the U.S. with devastating effects. Instantly vaporizing the people in the vicinity and causing long term radiation poisoning for the people not in the immediate blast range. Then they would go on to use another on Nagasaki three days later. After World War II Russia was able to create their own nuclear weapons witch thrust Russia and the U.S. into the cold war, due to their rivaling economic policies, in which due to fear of nuclear attack they would indirectly attack the other by supporting groups against them. Eventually this nuclear stockpile got so tremendously high, action needed to be taken. So they made a treaty to decrease the amount of nuclear warheads they had.
